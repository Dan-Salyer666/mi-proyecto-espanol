La regla de «De + Adjetivo»

In Spanish, when you want to describe an indefinite amount of a quality, you cannot attach an adjective directly to indefinite pronouns. You must use the preposition de to bridge the gap. This is highly common with verbs that denote possession or existence, specifically tener (to have) and hay (there is/there are).

①
Las palabras desencadenantes
THE TRIGGER WORDS
[Verb: Tener / Hay] + [Pronoun] + de + [Adjective]

This rule is triggered by five specific words that represent an indefinite "amount" or "thing". These words sit in the "pronoun slot" as the direct object of the verb.

  • Qué (What) — Used for questions/interrogations.
  • Algo (Something) — Used for affirmative statements.
  • Nada (Nothing) — Used for negative statements.
  • Mucho (A lot / Much) — Used for quantification.
  • Poco (Little) — Used for quantification.
②
El uso de «Qué»
UNDERSTANDING THE "PRONOUN SLOT"

To understand how qué works, we must look at how it replaces algo. They do the exact same grammatical job: they act as the direct object of the verb (what is being "had").

  • El libro tiene algo de interesante. The book has something interesting about it. — Affirmative: The book possesses an unknown "something" (algo).
  • ¿Qué tiene de interesante el libro? What is interesting about the book? — Interrogative: "algo" is replaced with "qué" and moved to the front.

Conclusion: Qué is not an invisible addition. It completely occupies the "pronoun slot," acting as the object that possesses the adjective through the bridge de.

③
Ejemplos con «Tener»
EXAMPLES USING TENER (TO HAVE)

When used with tener, this structure expresses what qualities a subject possesses. It translates best to English as "What does [subject] have that is [adjective]?" or "What is [adjective] about [subject]?"

  • ¿Qué tiene de malo el plan? What is bad about the plan? — (What does it have of bad?)
  • ¿Qué tienen de especial estas herramientas? What is special about these tools?
  • Esta ciudad tiene algo de mágico. This city has something magical about it.
  • La historia tiene algo de cierto. The story has some truth to it. — (something of true)
  • El diseño no tiene nada de raro. There is nothing weird about the design.
  • Esta situación no tiene nada de gracioso. There is nothing funny about this situation.
  • Ese argumento tiene mucho de válido. That argument is very valid. — (has much of valid)
  • La película tiene poco de original. The movie has very little originality. — (little of original)
④
Ejemplos con «Hay»
EXAMPLES USING HAY (THERE IS / THERE ARE)

When used with hay, the structure expresses the existence of a quality within a general setting or subject. It translates best to English as "There is [pronoun] [adjective] about..."

  • ¿Qué hay de nuevo? What's new? — (What is there of new?)
  • ¿Qué hay de bueno en la televisión hoy? What is good on TV today?
  • Hay algo de triste en su mirada. There is something sad in his/her look.
  • Hay algo de peligroso en este viaje. There is something dangerous about this trip.
  • No hay nada de malo en preguntar. There is nothing wrong with asking.
  • No hay nada de extraño aquí. There is nothing strange here.
  • Hay mucho de verdad en lo que dices. There is a lot of truth in what you say.
  • Hay poco de divertido en lavar los platos. There is little fun in washing the dishes.
