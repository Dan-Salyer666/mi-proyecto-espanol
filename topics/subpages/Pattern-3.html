Patrón 3

The Impression Pattern

✧
Expressing how things "turn out" or "come across"

This pattern bridges the logic of a standard active verb and a gustar-like reaction. A subject actively projects a vibe, fit, or outcome, but you (the indirect object) are the one receiving and judging that impression.

①
Core Mechanicsintransitive vessels
RECEIVING AN OUTCOME
(Subject) + [IOP] + Intransitive Verb + Adverb/Adjective

Unlike Gustar verbs, these verbs are emotionally neutral "vessels" (to fall, to fit, to seem). They require an adverb or adjective (bien, mal, perfecto) to complete the thought. The verb conjugates to match the subject projecting the impression.

  • Tú me caes bien. I like you. — Literally: You fall well to me. "Tú" is the subject driving the verb "caes".
  • Me parece una buena idea. It seems like a good idea to me. — The idea is the subject doing the seeming.
②
Social Impressionscaer & parecer
JUDGING VIBES & PEOPLE

Caer is the standard verb for expressing if you like a person's personality. (Do not use gustar for this, as gustar with people implies romantic or physical attraction). Parecer is used to express your opinion on an idea or situation.

  • El nuevo jefe nos cae mal. We don't like the new boss. — The boss "falls poorly" upon us.
  • A mi madre le cae bien mi novia. My mother likes my girlfriend. — My girlfriend is the subject projecting a good impression onto my mother.
  • Ese comentario me pareció raro. That comment seemed strange to me.
③
Outcomes & Fitquedar, ir, salir
HOW THINGS FARED

These verbs describe the final result of a situation, project, or piece of clothing from your personal perspective. The clothing or the event is the subject.

  • La camisa me queda chica. The shirt fits me small. — The shirt is the subject doing the fitting.
  • ¿Cómo te fue en el viaje? How did the trip go for you? — The trip is the subject; you are the receiver of the experience.
  • El asado me salió perfecto. The barbecue turned out perfect for me. — Used to describe the result of something you made or organized.
Nota · Uso Rioplatense In Argentina and Uruguay, Pattern 3 verbs are heavily modified by regional slang and voseo.

• Vos as the subject: Instead of "Tú me caes bien," you will hear "Vos me caes bien."
• Intensifiers: The adverb "bien" is frequently replaced with bárbaro (great) or intensified with re.
Example: "Esa camisa te queda bárbara." (That shirt looks great on you).
Example: "Vos me caes re bien." (I really like you).
