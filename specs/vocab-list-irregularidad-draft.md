> **What this becomes:** a third title-card badge **📖 Vocab** opening a modal that lists **every verb** on the page, with the **transitive** and **pronominal** forms shown separately (the pronominal noted as the resulting **state**). Each verb's headword carries a **desktop-only hover tooltip** showing its conjugation: *regular*, *regular (ortográfico)*, or *irregular* + what's irregular.
>
> **Review focus:** (1) the transitive/pronominal split and the state-glosses; (2) the **Conjugación** column — especially the ⚠ judgment calls. Correct here, then I build the modal.
>
> **Notes:** the modal will carry a small line — *"Pasa el cursor sobre cada verbo para ver su conjugación (función de escritorio)."* Verbs that appear on more than one card (*volcar, descomponerse, dañarse, estropearse, echar a perder*) are listed **once** here.

---

### Conjugation tiers (the tooltip text)
- **regular** — nothing surprising.
- **regular (ortográfico)** — only a spelling adjustment to keep the sound (*c→qu*, *z→c*). Not a true irregularity.
- **irregular** — real stem/participle/accent change, with the specific note.

⚠ = a call I want you to confirm (mostly the accent-shift verbs).

### The two ⚠ to confirm
1. **arruinar** — RAE accepts both *arruíno* (hiatus) and *arruino* (diphthong). I've called it **regular** with the accent noted. If you'd rather flag it as irregular accentuation, say so.
2. **averiar** — conjugates like *enviar* (*se avería*, *averío*), which is an accent irregularity. I've marked it **irregular (acento)**. If you consider that too fine a point, I'll downgrade to *regular*.

Everything else is either plainly regular, a clean spelling change (*choqué, destrocé*), or a clear stem/participle irregular (*roto, quiebro, descompuesto, podrido, demuele, destruyo, enmohezco, corroído*).
