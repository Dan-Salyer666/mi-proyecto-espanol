Los Verbos de Demostración

A structural breakdown of the verbs used to display, direct, and instruct, with a specific focus on Rioplatense regional usage.

①
Mostrar el acto de exhibir
To Display / To Manifest

The broadest and most common verb in the category. Mostrar is used when you are bringing an object or concept into someone's immediate field of vision or awareness. It handles both the physical display of objects and the abstract display of emotions or states.

  • ¿Me mostrás ese mate de calabaza? Will you show me that gourd mate? — Standard Rioplatense request in a shop.
  • El paciente no muestra síntomas de infección. The patient shows no symptoms of infection. — Abstract/clinical usage.
②
Enseñar el acto de instruir
To Teach / To Instruct

While often taught as a synonym for mostrar in textbooks, its mechanical function is to impart knowledge or demonstrate a process. Outside of Spain and Mexico, it is strictly bound to the concept of education or training.

Nota: Uso Rioplatense
In Buenos Aires and throughout Argentina, the division between mostrar and enseñar is absolute. Do not use enseñar to mean "show me an object." Reserve it entirely for requests regarding instruction or learning a new skill.
  • Él me enseñó a preparar un buen asado. He taught me how to prepare a good barbecue.
  • Estoy buscando a alguien que me enseñe el dialecto local. I am looking for someone to teach me the local dialect.
③
Señalar el acto de apuntar
To Point Out / To Highlight

Derived from the noun señal (a sign/signal). Use señalar when directing someone's eyes toward an object that already exists in the environment, rather than bringing the object to them. It is the mechanical equivalent of pointing a finger, and is also used to highlight specific facts in dialogue.

  • Me señaló la estación de subte desde la esquina. He pointed out the subway station to me from the corner.
  • El autor señala un problema importante en el código. The author points out an important problem in the code.
④
Indicar el acto de guiar
To Indicate / To Direct

The polite, logistical cousin to señalar. While pointing is purely physical, indicar provides procedural information or guidance. This is the optimal verb to use when asking locals for directions or clarifying a logistical process.

  • ¿Me podría indicar cómo llegar a la Plaza de Mayo? Could you show me (direct me) how to get to the Plaza de Mayo?
  • El manual indica que debemos reiniciar el sistema. The manual indicates that we must restart the system.
⑤
Presentar y Exhibir el contexto formal
To Present / To Exhibit

These verbs do not change the physical mechanics of showing, but elevate the formality. Presentar is strictly for showing documents to authorities or checkpoints. Exhibir is for passive public display, commonly seen in retail or museum contexts.

  • Por favor, presente su pasaporte en la aduana. Please present your passport at customs. — Official requirement.
  • Los artículos de cuero están exhibidos en la vidriera. The leather goods are displayed in the shop window. — Passive display.
