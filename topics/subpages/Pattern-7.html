Patrón 7

The Compound Pattern

❧
Stacking verbs for obligation, intent, and continuity

Periphrastic structures occur when two verbs work together to form a single idea. The first verb is a conjugated "helper" that establishes the tense and person. The second verb is left in its raw form (infinitive or gerund) to deliver the actual action.

①
Obligationtener que
REQUIREMENTS & DUTIES
Tener (Conjugated) + que + Infinitive

The equivalent of "to have to" in English. The verb tener establishes who holds the obligation, and the infinitive defines what the obligation is.

  • Tengo que estudiar hoy. I have to study today. — "Tengo" sets the subject; "estudiar" delivers the action.
  • Ellos tienen que trabajar. They have to work.
②
Future Intentir a
INFORMAL FUTURE TENSE
Ir (Conjugated) + a + Infinitive

The equivalent of "going to" in English. This is the most common way to express future plans in daily conversation, largely replacing the formal future tense.

  • Voy a salir temprano. I am going to leave early. — "Voy" sets the subject; "salir" is the planned action.
  • ¿Qué van a comer? What are they going to eat?
③
Continuous Stateestar + gerund
ACTIONS IN PROGRESS
Estar (Conjugated) + Gerund (-ando / -iendo)

The equivalent of the "-ing" form in English. The verb estar places the subject in the exact moment, and the gerund describes the ongoing action.

  • Estamos caminando. We are walking. — Emphasizes that the action is happening right now.
  • Ella está leyendo un libro. She is reading a book.
Nota · Uso Rioplatense When applying compound patterns under the Rioplatense voseo, you must apply the regional conjugation specifically to the "helper" verb.

• Tener: "Tú tienes que salir" becomes "Vos tenés que salir".
• Estar: "Tú estás hablando" becomes "Vos estás hablando". (The spelling is the same, but remember the emphasis is on the final syllable: estás).
• Ir: "Tú vas a salir" becomes "Vos vas a salir". (No change from standard).
